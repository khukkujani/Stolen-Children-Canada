Over 150,000 Indigenous children across Canada were forcibly taken from their families and interned in residential schools.

Duncan Scott, Dep. Minister of Indigenous Affairs 1920, stated: “I want to get rid of the Indian problem and continue until there is not a single left unabsorbed in Canada.

Cowessess First Nation have found the remains of 751 people, mostly children, at the former Marieval Indian Residential School.

On December 10, 2021, Minister Miller issued a statement committing to a new approach for federal document disclosure related to Residential Schools.

It’s not just a part of who we are as Survivors, it’s a part of who we are as a nation. – Murray Sinclair

This site aims to educate and explore the history and impacts of Canada’s Residential School System through Survivor stories, focus on healing the impacts of the Residential Schools through access to resources, keeping up with the latest news and trying to create an active community. Here, Survivors, their families, educators, researchers, and the public can examine the residential school system more deeply with the goal of fostering reconciliation and healing.

Get Involved

How to contribute or take action?

Ask a Teacher
Include this topic in every school curriculum. Provide materials to help educators teach youth and create awareness about the impacts on generations from the Residential School System.
Spread the Word
Talk about the impacts of the Residential School System on seven generations of Indigenous Peoples with your family, friends, and colleagues. Dispel myths or stereotypes that are discriminating. Speak out against racism whenever you see or hear it.
Be an ally!
Write a letter to elected officials (municipal, provincial, federal). Ask what they will do to support education, equality in programs and services to improve the lives of Indigenous Peoples. Demand action now!
Participate
Take part in Indigenous events or contact an Indigenous organizations to partner with you or your workplace and commit to creating a brighter future for generations to come.
Donate
to an Indigenous charities so they can produce more educational resources that raise awareness and promote respect among all people in Canada.
